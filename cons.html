AI in Education Blog

Reduced Human Interaction

Heavy tech use can weaken interpersonal learning moments and mentor relationships.

Privacy Risks

Large-scale student data collection creates potential for misuse and profiling without safeguards.

Access Inequality

Not all learners have devices or reliable connectivity; inequality can widen if unaddressed.

Algorithmic Bias

Poorly trained models may reinforce existing biases and unfairly affect some students.

Over-reliance on Technology

Students might become dependent on AI tools, reducing critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

High Implementation Costs

Integrating AI in education requires investment in software, hardware, and training, which may be a barrier for some institutions.
